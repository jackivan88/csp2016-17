Taking Screenshots

You'll have to take screenshots of your work throughout this course, so it's important to learn how to do it quickly and correctly. If you're using macOS, then you have some tools built in that we'll use. If you're using Windows, you need to install ShareX since the built-in Windows screenshot tools aren't quite good enough for what we need to do. Go to the section for your operating system and complete the checkpoints.

  • macOS
    • Checkpoint 1: Full Screen
    • Checkpoint 2: Selection
    • Checkpoint 3: Window
    • Checkpoint 4: Full Screen Recording
    • Checkpoint 5: Partial Screen Recording
  • Windows
    • Checkpoint 1: Full Screen
    • Checkpoint 2: Selection
    • Checkpoint 3: Window
    • Checkpoint 4: Full Screen Recording
    • Checkpoint 5: Partial Screen Recording

macOS

Before you do anything else, read this article published by Apple that explains basic screenshot keyboard shortcuts.

Checkpoint 1: Full Screen

Take a screenshot of your entire screen by pressing Command-Shift-3. Find the file on your desktop and rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint1.png and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Checkpoint 2: Selection

Take a screenshot of only part of your screen by pressing Command-Shift-4. Find the file on your desktop and rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint2.png and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Checkpoint 3: Window

Open Finder and then screenshot only that window by pressing Command-Shift-4 and then the Space bar. Find the file on your desktop and rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint3.png and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Checkpoint 4: Full Screen Recording

Before moving on, read this article published by Apple that explains how to record your screen using Quicktime.

Open Quicktime and then go to the File menu and choose New Screen Recording and click anywhere on the screen. Record your screen for five to ten seconds, press Command-Control-Escape, and then save the file to your desktop. Find the file on your desktop and rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint4.mov and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Checkpoint 5: Partial Screen Recording

Open Quicktime and then go to the File menu and choose New Screen Recording and then click and drag the mouse to select part of the screen. Record your selection for five to ten seconds, press Command-Control-Escape, and then save the file to your desktop. Find the file on your desktop and rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint5.mov and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Turn in your checkpoint sheet to the assignment basket once it is completely signed. You may now work on stretch projects.

Windows

If you installed the standard student software pack, you already have ShareX installed. If you removed it, you should reinstall the student software pack now.

Checkpoint 1: Full Screen

Take a screenshot of your entire screen by pressing the Print Screen button on your keyboard. Find the file in your ShareX folder, rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint1.png and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Checkpoint 2: Selection

Take a screenshot of only part of your screen by pressing Control-Print Screen and clicking and dragging to select a region. Find the file in your ShareX folder, rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint2.png and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Checkpoint 3: Window

Open Windows Explorer and then screenshot only that window by pressing Control-Print Screen and then hovering over that window and clicking. Find the file in your ShareX folder, rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint3.png and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Checkpoint 4: Full Screen Recording

Press Shift-Print Screen and make sure you select the entire screen. If it asks you to install FFMPEG, do so. Record your screen for five to ten seconds and then press Shift-Print Screen again. Find the file in your ShareX folder and rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint4.mp4 and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Checkpoint 5: Partial Screen Recording

Press Shift-Print Screen and make sure you select only a part of your screen. Record your selection for five to ten seconds and then press Shift-Print Screen again. Find the file in your ShareX folder and rename it takingScreenshotsCheckpoint5.mp4 and attach it to the Taking Screenshots assignment on Google Classroom.

Raise your hand and have Mr. Olinda verify this checkpoint.

Turn in your checkpoint sheet to the assignment basket once it is completely signed. You may now work on stretch projects.
