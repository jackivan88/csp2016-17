당신의 컴퓨터의 운영체제 사용하기

당신의 컴퓨터의 운영체제는 맥 OS나 윈도우입니다. 이 레슨에서 당신은 더 빠르고 효과적으로 일할 수 있게 당신의 운영체제의 기본적인 features를 배울 것입니다.

일단, 당신은 당신이 사용하고 있는 운영체제와 그 버전 이 무엇인지 알아야 합니다. 맥 OS로 운영하는 거의 모든 컴퓨터들은 애플에서 제조되고, 모든 애플 컴퓨터들은 애플의 로고가 컴퓨터의 어딘가에 있습니다. 그러나, 애플 컴퓨터들은 맥 OS가 아닌 운영체제를 사용하는 것이 가능합니다. 하드웨어는 소프트웨어와 다르단 것을 항상 기억하세요. 하드웨어는 소프트웨어가 사용되는 기계입니다.

당신의 스크린의 위쪽 끝에 사과가 왼쪽 끝에 있는 메뉴 바가 보인다면, 당신은 맥 OS를 사용하고 있습니다. 만약 당신이 스크린의 밑쪽 끝을 봤을 때 네 개로 나뉘어진 네모나 동그라미가 보인다면, 당신은 윈도우를 사용하고 있습니다. 그러나, 맥 OS와 윈도우는 많은 버전들이 있으니, 당신의 컴퓨터가 무엇을 운영하고 있는지 더 배우기 위해 알맞은 부분을 찾아서 체크포인트들을 따라가세요.

Checkpoint 2: 잠금과 잠금 해제

By default, Apple computers don't have a quick way to lock the screen when you move away from your computer. However, you should know how to enable this so that your computer is always secure when you're not using it. Before you do anything, try pressing Control-Shift-Eject (if you have a CD drive) or Control-Shift-Power (if you do not have a CD drive) and see what happens.

Then, go to the Apple menu and choose System Preferences. Choose Security & Privacy and look under the General tab. You should see a check box labeled Require password... Choose an interval and then close your System Preferences. If you did this correctly, then pressing Control-Shift-Eject or Control-Shift-Power should cause your screen to go black. When you move the cursor or hit a key, it should require you to enter your password to use your computer.

Go to the Apple menu and find the option labeled Sleep. Press it and see what happens. Then move the cursor or press a key to wake it back up. Then close the laptop, leave it for about five seconds, and open it back up. Which method would work on either a desktop or a laptop?

Checkpoint 8: 일반적인 단축키들

Your Apple computer has several modifier keys. These keys don't do anything on their own (you can try pressing one to see this) but when they are pressed along with other keys they allow you to perform different tasks.

  • Command ⌘
  • Shift ⇧
  • Option ⌥
  • Control ⌃
  • Caps Lock ⇪
  • Fn

You should know these modifier keys and be able to press the right one when asked. Take a look at the first section of Apple's list of Mac keyboard shortcuts (titled Cut, copy, paste, and other common shortcuts) and try each one of them. One of the most important shortcuts is Command-Tab for switching between windows. You must learn to use this instead of the mouse. Once you're done you will be asked to try several at random. Once you can do at least five from memory you'll be able to move on.

Windows

Checkpoint 1: Which Version?

First, press Windows-X on your keyboard. A menu should appear in the lower-left corner of your screen. Choose System. This will bring up your computer's information. See if you can find the name of your operating system. If nothing happened when you pressed Windows-X, then click the Windows icon at the lower-left corner of your screen. On the right-hand side of the Start Menu you'll see Computer as one of the choices. Right-click on it and select Properties. You should be able to see the name of your operating system.

Checkpoint 2: Lock and Unlock

To lock your laptop, press Windows-L. This will immediately lock the laptop. If you have a password, type it in to get back into your account. If you don't have a password, you need to create one by going to your settings (Windows-I) and editing your user account. This will keep your computer secure when you are not using it.

Go to the Start Menu and click on your user name at the top. You should see an option to lock you laptop. Choose it and then unlock the computer again.

Windows has several modifier keys. They change the behavior of the other keys on your keyboard although the Windows key also works all by itself.

  • Caps Lock

  • Alt

  • Fn

You should know each of the modifier keys and be able to press the right one when asked. This article contains the most important shortcuts for Windows 10. Try each of them out and make sure you know what they do. One of the most important shortcuts is Alt-Tab for switching between windows. You must learn to use this instead of the mouse.
